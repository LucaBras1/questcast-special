The forest goes silent. Birds stop singing. You kneel beside a fresh track in the wet earth. A large paw print, deep impression, claws like daggers. Something big passed through here moments ago. Wind carries the scent of blood and wet fur. Your hand drifts to your bow. Between the trees you catch movement. Two yellow eyes watch you from the thicket. Not an enemy. Not yet. The creature waits for your next move. The forest holds its breath.
